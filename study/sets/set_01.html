Q.No 21 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Which body recommends the distribution of taxes between Center and States?
A
Planning Commission
B
Finance Commission
✓ Correct
C
Parliament
D
NITI Aayog

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Finance Commission, a constitutional body under Article 280, is constituted every five years by the President to recommend how the net proceeds of taxes should be divided (vertical devolution) between the Union and the States, and how funds should be distributed among the States (horizontal devolution).

A is incorrect: The Planning Commission (1950-2014) prepared Five-Year Plans and allocated plan assistance, but it had no role in dividing tax revenue and is now defunct.

C is incorrect: Parliament enacts tax laws and passes the Budget, but it does not recommend the formula for sharing tax proceeds with the States.

D is incorrect: NITI Aayog is only an advisory think-tank that replaced the Planning Commission; it has no constitutional power over tax devolution.

💡 The 15th Finance Commission (chaired by N.K. Singh) recommended a 41% share of the divisible pool for the States.
Q.No 22 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Who appoints the chairman and members of the Finance Commission?
A
Parliament
B
Union Cabinet
C
President
✓ Correct
D
Prime Minister

Solution

Correct answer: C. Under Article 280, the President of India constitutes the Finance Commission and appoints its Chairman and the other members, along with deciding their qualifications and manner of selection.

A is incorrect: Parliament only enacts the law laying down qualifications of members; it does not appoint them.

B is incorrect: The Union Cabinet may advise the President, but the formal appointing authority is the President.

D is incorrect: The Prime Minister heads the Council of Ministers but does not appoint the Finance Commission.

💡 A Finance Commission has a Chairman and four other members.
Q.No 23 Art & CultureEnglish
During which festival is Gaur dance predominantly performed?
A
Diwali
B
Holi
✓ Correct
C
Navratri
D
Baisakhi

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Gaur (bison-horn) dance is a tribal dance of the Maria Gonds of the Bastar region (Chhattisgarh/Madhya Pradesh) and is performed prominently during festive/spring celebrations associated with Holi.

A is incorrect: Diwali is the festival of lights celebrated with lamps and fireworks, not specifically with the Gaur dance.

C is incorrect: Navratri is associated with Garba and Dandiya of Gujarat, not the Gaur dance.

D is incorrect: Baisakhi is a Punjabi harvest festival celebrated with the Bhangra and Gidda dances.

💡 Dancers wear headdresses made of bison (gaur) horns, which gives the dance its name.

Q.No 25 Science & TechnologyEnglish
When is acceleration considered positive?
A
When it is opposite to velocity
B
When velocity is decreasing
C
When it is in the direction of velocity
✓ Correct
D
When the object is at rest

Solution

Correct answer: C. Acceleration is taken as positive when it acts in the same direction as the velocity, because the body then speeds up in its direction of motion.

A is incorrect: When acceleration is opposite to velocity it is negative acceleration (retardation/deceleration).

B is incorrect: Decreasing velocity means the acceleration opposes motion, i.e. it is negative.

D is incorrect: An object at rest with no change in velocity has zero acceleration.

💡 Acceleration is a vector quantity, so its sign depends on the chosen positive direction relative to the velocity.

Q.No 27 EconomyEnglish
The Ease of Doing Business (EODB) indicator “Enforcing Contracts” measures:
A
implementation of labor regulations
B
Ease of paying and managing taxes
C
Efficiency of the judicial system in resolving commercial disputes
✓ Correct
D
Effectiveness of law enforcement agencies

Solution

Correct answer: C. In the World Bank's Ease of Doing Business framework, the 'Enforcing Contracts' indicator measures the time, cost and procedural quality of the judicial system in resolving a standardised commercial dispute.

A is incorrect: Labour-regulation implementation is captured by separate labour-market indicators, not Enforcing Contracts.

B is incorrect: Ease of paying and managing taxes is measured by the 'Paying Taxes' indicator.

D is incorrect: Effectiveness of law-enforcement (police) agencies is a governance/security measure, not part of this EODB indicator.

💡 The World Bank discontinued the Doing Business report in 2021 and is replacing it with the Business Ready (B-READY) project.

Q.No 29 Science & TechnologyEnglish
if a spacecraft travels at 30 km/s, how long will it take to cover 1 AU (150 million km)?
A
15 days
B
58 days
✓ Correct
C
100 days
D
365 days

Solution

Correct answer: B. Time = distance / speed = 150,000,000 km / 30 km/s = 5,000,000 seconds, which is about 57.9 days, i.e. roughly 58 days.

A is incorrect: 15 days would require a speed of about 116 km/s.

C is incorrect: 100 days corresponds to roughly 17 km/s.

D is incorrect: 365 days (one year) corresponds to a speed of under 5 km/s.

💡 1 AU is the average Earth-Sun distance; light covers it in about 8 minutes 20 seconds.

Q.No 31 EconomyEnglish
What was the main drawback of small-scale industry reservation policies?
A
They encouraged monopoly power
B
They eliminated export opportunities
C
They excluded rural entrepreneurs
D
They restricted economies of scale and technological upgrading
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. Reserving products exclusively for small-scale industries kept firms artificially small, which restricted economies of scale and discouraged investment in modern technology, hurting productivity and competitiveness.

A is incorrect: The policy was meant to prevent monopoly by protecting small firms, not to encourage monopoly power.

B is incorrect: It did not specifically eliminate export opportunities; the core problem was scale and technology.

C is incorrect: Reservation did not exclude rural entrepreneurs; in fact small units were widely spread.

💡 India progressively 'de-reserved' SSI products after 1997, and the reservation list was abolished entirely by 2015.
Q.No 32 EconomyEnglish
Which was the model for the First Five-Year Plan (1951-1956)?
A
Solow model
B
Harrod-Domar model
✓ Correct
C
Keynesian model
D
Mahalanobis model

Solution

Correct answer: B. India's First Five-Year Plan (1951-56) was based on the Harrod-Domar model, which links growth to the rate of saving and investment and the capital-output ratio.

A is incorrect: The Solow growth model came later and was not the basis of the First Plan.

C is incorrect: The Keynesian model focuses on demand management, not the planning framework used here.

D is incorrect: The Mahalanobis model was the basis of the Second Five-Year Plan (1956-61), not the First.

💡 The First Plan prioritised agriculture and irrigation following the disruptions of Partition.

To whom did the rebel soldiers of the Meerut Regiment want to hand over the rule of the country?
A
Bahadur Shah Zafar
✓ Correct
B
Peshwa Baji Rao Il

Solution
