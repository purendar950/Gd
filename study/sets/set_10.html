Q.No 27 GeographyEnglish
Which of the following plate boundaries formed the Himalayas?
A
Divergent boundary
B
Transform boundary
C
Hotspot boundary
D
Convergent boundary
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. The Himalayas were formed at a convergent plate boundary, where the Indian Plate collided with and pushed under the Eurasian Plate.

A is incorrect: At a divergent boundary plates move apart, forming rifts and mid-ocean ridges.

B is incorrect: At a transform boundary plates slide past each other (e.g. the San Andreas Fault).

C is incorrect: A hotspot creates volcanic chains (like Hawaii), not fold mountains like the Himalayas.

💡 The Indian Plate is still pushing north, so the Himalayas continue to rise by a few millimetres each year.
Q.No 28 Science & TechnologyEnglish
Why are the days on Venus longer than its years?
A
Venus has a slower rotation than revolution
✓ Correct
B
Venus revolves very slowly around the Sun
C
Venus does not rotate
D
Venus spins on its axis in the opposite direction

Solution

Correct answer: A. A day on Venus is longer than its year because Venus rotates on its axis very slowly - its single rotation (day) takes longer than one revolution around the Sun (year).

B is incorrect: Venus actually revolves around the Sun faster than it spins, which is why the year is shorter.

C is incorrect: Venus does rotate, just extremely slowly.

D is incorrect: Venus does spin in the opposite (retrograde) direction, but the day-longer-than-year fact is due to its slow rotation.

💡 One Venus day equals about 243 Earth days, while a Venus year is about 225 Earth days.

Muscular tissue
B
Nervous tissue
C
Connective tissue
✓ Correct
D
Epithelial tissue

Solution

Correct answer: C. Blood is classified as connective tissue because its cells are suspended in a fluid matrix (plasma) and it connects and transports materials throughout the body.

Q.No 34 Science & TechnologyEnglish
Which of the following is an example of chemotropism in plants?
A
Shoot bending towards light
B
Root growing downwards
C
Pollen tube growing towards ovule
✓ Correct
D
Leaf folding on touch

Solution

Correct answer: C. A pollen tube growing towards the ovule (in response to chemical signals from the ovary) is an example of chemotropism - growth directed by a chemical stimulus.

A is incorrect: A shoot bending towards light is phototropism.

B is incorrect: A root growing downwards is geotropism (gravitropism).

D is incorrect: A leaf folding on touch (as in Mimosa) is thigmonasty/seismonasty, not chemotropism.

💡 Tropic movements are directional growth responses to stimuli such as light, gravity, water or chemicals.
