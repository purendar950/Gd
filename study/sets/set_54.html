Who decides the number of judges in a high court?
A
President of India
✓ Correct
B
Parliament
C
Chief Justice of India
D
Governor of the State

Solution

Correct answer: A. The President of India decides the number of judges in a High Court (the President appoints them and fixes the strength).

💡 High Court judges are appointed by the President under Article 217.

Q.No 24 Science & TechnologyEnglish
Why do action and reaction forces, though equal in magnitude, not always produce equal accelerations?
A
The forces act on the same object
B
The forces are in the same direction
C
The objects have different masses
✓ Correct
D
One force is stronger than the other

Solution

Correct answer: C. Action and reaction forces are equal in magnitude but produce different accelerations because the two objects have different masses (a = F/m).

A is incorrect: The forces act on different objects, not the same one.

B is incorrect: The forces are in opposite directions.

D is incorrect: The forces are equal in magnitude, not unequal.

💡 This follows from Newton's third law combined with the second law (a = F/m).
