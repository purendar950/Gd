Q.No 34 Schemes & GovernmentEnglish
What is the goal of the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) scheme launched in 2015?
A
Promote digital services
B
Expand rural employment
C
Enhance agricultural marketing
D
Urban infrastructure and water supply
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. AMRUT, launched in 2015, aims to provide basic urban infrastructure - especially assured water supply, sewerage, drainage and green spaces - in selected cities and towns.

A is incorrect: Promoting digital services is the focus of Digital India, not AMRUT.

B is incorrect: Expanding rural employment is the goal of MGNREGA.

C is incorrect: Agricultural marketing is addressed by schemes like e-NAM, not AMRUT.

💡 AMRUT 2.0 was launched in 2021 with the goal of universal water-supply coverage in all statutory towns.

वर्ष 202-22 से 2025-26 तक 450 करोड़ रुपये की राशि के लिए किस सामाजिक विकास योजना को मंजूरी दी गई है?
A
एस.एम.आई.एल.ई.योजना
B
पी.एम.-दक्ष योजना
✓ Correct
C
नमस्ते योजना
D
नशा मुक्त भारत अभियान

Solution

Correct answer: B. The PM-DAKSH (Pradhan Mantri Dakshta Aur Kushalta Sampann Hitgrahi) Yojana, a skilling scheme for marginalised groups, was approved with an outlay of about Rs 450 crore for 2021-22 to 2025-26.

A is incorrect: The SMILE scheme supports the welfare and rehabilitation of transgender persons and beggars, not this skilling outlay.

C is incorrect: The NAMASTE scheme is for the safety and dignity of sanitation workers.

D is incorrect: Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan is a drug-free India campaign, not a skilling scheme.

💡 PM-DAKSH targets SCs, OBCs, EWS, safai karamcharis and DNTs for free skill training, implemented by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment.
Q.No 34 Schemes & GovernmentEnglish
The Make in India initiative was launched in:
A
2010
B
2012
C
2014
✓ Correct
D
2015

Solution

Correct answer: C. The 'Make in India' initiative was launched in September 2014 to encourage companies to manufacture in India and to boost the manufacturing sector's share of the economy.

A is incorrect: 2010 pre-dates the initiative.

B is incorrect: 2012 is incorrect; the launch was in 2014.

D is incorrect: 2015 is the launch year of Digital India and several other schemes, not Make in India.

💡 The Make in India lion logo, made of gear wheels, symbolises manufacturing and national progress.
