Q.No 21 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Which body recommends the distribution of taxes between Center and States?
A
Planning Commission
B
Finance Commission
✓ Correct
C
Parliament
D
NITI Aayog

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Finance Commission, a constitutional body under Article 280, is constituted every five years by the President to recommend how the net proceeds of taxes should be divided (vertical devolution) between the Union and the States, and how funds should be distributed among the States (horizontal devolution).

A is incorrect: The Planning Commission (1950-2014) prepared Five-Year Plans and allocated plan assistance, but it had no role in dividing tax revenue and is now defunct.

C is incorrect: Parliament enacts tax laws and passes the Budget, but it does not recommend the formula for sharing tax proceeds with the States.

D is incorrect: NITI Aayog is only an advisory think-tank that replaced the Planning Commission; it has no constitutional power over tax devolution.

💡 The 15th Finance Commission (chaired by N.K. Singh) recommended a 41% share of the divisible pool for the States.
Q.No 22 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Who appoints the chairman and members of the Finance Commission?
A
Parliament
B
Union Cabinet
C
President
✓ Correct
D
Prime Minister

Solution

Correct answer: C. Under Article 280, the President of India constitutes the Finance Commission and appoints its Chairman and the other members, along with deciding their qualifications and manner of selection.

A is incorrect: Parliament only enacts the law laying down qualifications of members; it does not appoint them.

B is incorrect: The Union Cabinet may advise the President, but the formal appointing authority is the President.

D is incorrect: The Prime Minister heads the Council of Ministers but does not appoint the Finance Commission.

💡 A Finance Commission has a Chairman and four other members.

Reduce salaries of government employees
✓ Correct
B

C

Solution

A
President
B

D

✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. In a state, the Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to the State Legislative Assembly (the directly elected House), mirroring the Union arrangement with the Lok Sabha.
