Q.No 21 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Which body recommends the distribution of taxes between Center and States?
A
Planning Commission
B
Finance Commission
✓ Correct
C
Parliament
D
NITI Aayog

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Finance Commission, a constitutional body under Article 280, is constituted every five years by the President to recommend how the net proceeds of taxes should be divided (vertical devolution) between the Union and the States, and how funds should be distributed among the States (horizontal devolution).

A is incorrect: The Planning Commission (1950-2014) prepared Five-Year Plans and allocated plan assistance, but it had no role in dividing tax revenue and is now defunct.

C is incorrect: Parliament enacts tax laws and passes the Budget, but it does not recommend the formula for sharing tax proceeds with the States.

D is incorrect: NITI Aayog is only an advisory think-tank that replaced the Planning Commission; it has no constitutional power over tax devolution.

💡 The 15th Finance Commission (chaired by N.K. Singh) recommended a 41% share of the divisible pool for the States.
Q.No 22 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
Who appoints the chairman and members of the Finance Commission?
A
Parliament
B
Union Cabinet
C
President
✓ Correct
D
Prime Minister

Solution

Correct answer: C. Under Article 280, the President of India constitutes the Finance Commission and appoints its Chairman and the other members, along with deciding their qualifications and manner of selection.

A is incorrect: Parliament only enacts the law laying down qualifications of members; it does not appoint them.

B is incorrect: The Union Cabinet may advise the President, but the formal appointing authority is the President.

D is incorrect: The Prime Minister heads the Council of Ministers but does not appoint the Finance Commission.

💡 A Finance Commission has a Chairman and four other members.
Q.No 39 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
The PCPNDT Act was enacted by the Government of India in for prohibition of sex selection.
A
1991
B
1994
✓ Correct
C
2011
D
2015

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Pre-Conception and Pre-Natal Diagnostic Techniques (PCPNDT) Act was enacted in 1994 to prohibit sex selection and the misuse of diagnostic techniques for determining the sex of a foetus.

A is incorrect: 1991 is incorrect; the Act came three years later.

C is incorrect: 2011 was the year of a Census that highlighted the skewed child sex ratio, not the year of the Act.

D is incorrect: 2015 is much later; major amendments to the Act were made in 2003, not 2015.

💡 The Act was significantly amended in 2003 to also regulate pre-conception sex-selection techniques.
Q.No 21 Polity & ConstitutionHindi
केंद्रीय मंत्रिपरिषद में मंत्रियों की अधिकतम संख्या निम्नलिखित तक सीमित है:
A
10% of the Lok Sabha
B
12% of the Lok Sabha
C
15% of the Lok Sabha
✓ Correct
D
20% of the Lok Sabha

Solution

Correct answer: C. The 91st Constitutional Amendment (2003) inserted Article 75(1A), capping the total number of ministers in the Union Council of Ministers, including the Prime Minister, at 15% of the total strength of the Lok Sabha.

A is incorrect: 10% is below the constitutional ceiling.

B is incorrect: 12% is not the figure fixed by the 91st Amendment.

D is incorrect: 20% exceeds the limit; the cap is 15%.

💡 The same 91st Amendment also set a minimum of 12 ministers for smaller states and strengthened anti-defection rules.

✓ Correct
C
प्रधानमंत्री
D
सर्वोच्च न्यायालय

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Governor of a state sends a report to the President recommending President's Rule (Article 356) when the state government cannot function according to the Constitution.

Solution

Which body recommends the distribution of tax revenues between union and states?

NITI Aayog
B
Parliament
C
Finance Commission
✓ Correct
D
Planning Commission

Solution

Correct answer: C. The Finance Commission (Article 280) recommends the distribution of the net proceeds of taxes between the Union and the States and the principles governing grants-in-aid to the States.

Q.No 22 Polity & ConstitutionEnglish
During a financial emergency under Article 360, the president can:
A
Reduce salaries of government employees
✓ Correct
B
Dismiss State Governments
C
Dissolve Parliament
D
Suspend Fundamental Rights

Solution

Correct answer: A. During a Financial Emergency under Article 360, the President can direct the reduction of salaries and allowances of government employees, including judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts, to maintain financial stability.

B is incorrect: Dismissing state governments is a feature of President's Rule under Article 356, not a Financial Emergency.

C is incorrect: Dissolving Parliament is not a power granted under Article 360.

D is incorrect: Suspension of Fundamental Rights is linked to a National Emergency under Article 352, not a Financial Emergency.

💡 A Financial Emergency under Article 360 has never been declared in India.
