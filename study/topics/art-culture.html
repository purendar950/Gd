Q.No 23 Art & CultureEnglish
During which festival is Gaur dance predominantly performed?
A
Diwali
B
Holi
✓ Correct
C
Navratri
D
Baisakhi

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Gaur (bison-horn) dance is a tribal dance of the Maria Gonds of the Bastar region (Chhattisgarh/Madhya Pradesh) and is performed prominently during festive/spring celebrations associated with Holi.

A is incorrect: Diwali is the festival of lights celebrated with lamps and fireworks, not specifically with the Gaur dance.

C is incorrect: Navratri is associated with Garba and Dandiya of Gujarat, not the Gaur dance.

D is incorrect: Baisakhi is a Punjabi harvest festival celebrated with the Bhangra and Gidda dances.

💡 Dancers wear headdresses made of bison (gaur) horns, which gives the dance its name.

A

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Tippani folk dance of the Chorwad region of Gujarat is performed by women and is accompanied by percussion instruments such as the dhol and manjira (small cymbals), which set its rhythmic beat.

Q.No 23 Art & CultureEnglish
Why is the Raga Tarangini considered highly important in Hindustani music history?
A
It uses only southern raga's
B
It introduces tabla and harmonium
C
It uses many raga names still in use today
✓ Correct
D
It rejects traditional Indian theories

Solution

Correct answer: C. The Raga Tarangini is considered historically important because it records and uses many raga names that are still in use in Hindustani music today, providing continuity with earlier tradition.

A is incorrect: It does not deal only with southern (Carnatic) ragas; it is a Hindustani-music text.

B is incorrect: It is a treatise on ragas, not a work introducing the tabla and harmonium.

D is incorrect: It builds on, rather than rejects, traditional Indian musical theory.

💡 Raga Tarangini was authored by Locana (Lochana Kavi), an early text valuable for the history of ragas.
