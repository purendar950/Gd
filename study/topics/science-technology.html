Q.No 25 Science & TechnologyEnglish
When is acceleration considered positive?
A
When it is opposite to velocity
B
When velocity is decreasing
C
When it is in the direction of velocity
✓ Correct
D
When the object is at rest

Solution

Correct answer: C. Acceleration is taken as positive when it acts in the same direction as the velocity, because the body then speeds up in its direction of motion.

A is incorrect: When acceleration is opposite to velocity it is negative acceleration (retardation/deceleration).

B is incorrect: Decreasing velocity means the acceleration opposes motion, i.e. it is negative.

D is incorrect: An object at rest with no change in velocity has zero acceleration.

💡 Acceleration is a vector quantity, so its sign depends on the chosen positive direction relative to the velocity.
Q.No 29 Science & TechnologyEnglish
if a spacecraft travels at 30 km/s, how long will it take to cover 1 AU (150 million km)?
A
15 days
B
58 days
✓ Correct
C
100 days
D
365 days

Solution

Correct answer: B. Time = distance / speed = 150,000,000 km / 30 km/s = 5,000,000 seconds, which is about 57.9 days, i.e. roughly 58 days.

A is incorrect: 15 days would require a speed of about 116 km/s.

C is incorrect: 100 days corresponds to roughly 17 km/s.

D is incorrect: 365 days (one year) corresponds to a speed of under 5 km/s.

💡 1 AU is the average Earth-Sun distance; light covers it in about 8 minutes 20 seconds.
Q.No 24 Science & TechnologyHindi
चंद्रमा पर पृथ्वी की तुलना में कम गुरुत्वाकर्षण बल क्यों लगता है?
A
यह आकार में छोटा है
B
इसका द्रव्यमान कम है
✓ Correct
C
यह तेजी से घूमता है
D
यह सूर्य से अधिक दूर है

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Moon exerts less gravitational pull than the Earth mainly because it has much less mass (about 1/81 of Earth's mass); gravity depends on mass, so surface gravity on the Moon is about one-sixth of Earth's.

A is incorrect: Smaller size alone does not determine gravity; it is the mass that matters, though the Moon is indeed smaller.

C is incorrect: The Moon actually rotates slowly (tidally locked), and rotation speed does not reduce gravity.

D is incorrect: Distance from the Sun is irrelevant to the Moon's own surface gravity.

💡 Surface gravity g = GM/R^2; the Moon's lower mass M is the dominant reason its g is about 1.62 m/s^2.

Q.No 24 Science & TechnologyHindi
यदि दो वस्तुओं के बीच की दूरी छह गुना बढ़ जाए तो गुरुत्वाकर्षण बल पर क्या प्रभाव पड़ेगा?
A
36 गुना बड़ा हो जाता है
B
6 गुना बड़ा हो जाता है
C
36 गुना छोटा हो जाता है
✓ Correct
D
/6 गुना छोटा हो जाता है

Solution

Correct answer: C. By Newton's law of gravitation, force is inversely proportional to the square of the distance. If the distance increases 6 times, the force becomes 1/6^2 = 1/36, i.e. 36 times smaller.

A is incorrect: Force decreases, not increases, when distance grows, so '36 times larger' is wrong.

B is incorrect: Force does not increase 6 times; it decreases with the square of distance.

D is incorrect: Force falls by the square (1/36), not just 1/6.

💡 Newton's law: F = G m1 m2 / r^2, the inverse-square law of gravitation.

Q.No 24 Science & TechnologyEnglish
Archimedes’ principle states that a body immersed in a fluid experiences an upward force equal to
A
Its own weight
B
The volume of the body
C
The weight of the fluid displaced
✓ Correct
D
The density of the fluid

Solution

Correct answer: C. Archimedes' principle states that a body immersed (fully or partly) in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces.

A is incorrect: The buoyant force equals the displaced fluid's weight, not the body's own weight (these are equal only when the body floats).

B is incorrect: The volume of the body is a quantity, not a force.

D is incorrect: Density of the fluid alone is not a force; the upthrust depends on weight of fluid displaced.

💡 This principle explains why ships float and why objects feel lighter in water.

Q.No 32 Science & TechnologyEnglish⚠ low OCR confidence
if an astronaut’s mass is 70 kg, what will be their weight on the Moon (1/6th of Earth's gravity)?
A
700 N
B
117 N
✓ Correct
C
420 N
D
686 N

Solution

Correct answer: B. Weight on the Moon = mass x lunar gravity = 70 kg x (9.8/6) m/s^2 ≈ 70 x 1.63 ≈ 114-117 N.

A is incorrect: 700 N (using g = 10) would be the approximate weight on Earth, not the Moon.

C is incorrect: 420 N would imply gravity far higher than one-sixth of Earth's.

D is incorrect: 686 N (70 x 9.8) is the weight on Earth, not on the Moon.

💡 Mass stays constant everywhere; only weight (= mass x local gravity) changes between the Earth and the Moon.
