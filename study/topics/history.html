Q.No 36 HistoryEnglish
Who mentioned the role of the Banjaras in transporting grain in his memoirs?
A
Babur
B
Humayun
C
Sher Shah Suri
D
Jahangir
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. Emperor Jahangir, in his memoirs (the Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri / Jahangirnama), described the Banjaras as itinerant traders who transported grain on bullocks across the country.

A is incorrect: Babur's memoirs (Baburnama) focus on his conquests and observations of India, not the Banjaras' grain trade.

B is incorrect: Humayun did not leave such memoirs; his life was recorded by others (e.g. the Humayun-nama by Gulbadan Begum).

C is incorrect: Sher Shah Suri is known for administrative reforms and the Grand Trunk Road, not for memoirs on the Banjaras.

💡 The Banjaras were a mobile trading community who moved goods in large caravans called 'tandas'.
Q.No 37 HistoryEnglish
What term was used to describe the student stage of life in early Hindu philosophy?
A
Vanaprastha
B
Brahmachari
✓ Correct
C
Sannyasin
D
Grihastha

Solution

Correct answer: B. In the ancient Hindu ashrama system, 'Brahmacharya' was the first stage of life - the celibate student stage devoted to learning under a guru. A student was called a Brahmachari.

A is incorrect: Vanaprastha is the third stage - gradual retirement and withdrawal from worldly life.

C is incorrect: Sannyasin refers to the fourth stage - renunciation of all worldly attachments.

D is incorrect: Grihastha is the second stage - the householder/family life.

💡 The four ashramas are Brahmacharya, Grihastha, Vanaprastha and Sannyasa.
Q.No 40 HistoryEnglish
To whom did the rebel soldiers of the Meerut Regiment want to hand over the rule of the country?
A
Bahadur Shah Zafar
✓ Correct
B
Peshwa Baji Rao Il
C
Nana Saheb
D
Rani Lakshmibai

Solution

Correct answer: A. The rebel sepoys of the Meerut regiment marched to Delhi in May 1857 and proclaimed the aged Mughal emperor Bahadur Shah Zafar II as the symbolic ruler of Hindustan.

B is incorrect: Peshwa Baji Rao II had died in 1851; it was his adopted heir Nana Saheb who led the revolt at Kanpur.

C is incorrect: Nana Saheb led the rebellion in Kanpur, but the Meerut sepoys went to Delhi to the Mughal emperor.

D is incorrect: Rani Lakshmibai led the revolt at Jhansi, not the figure the Meerut soldiers turned to.

💡 After the revolt was crushed, Bahadur Shah Zafar was exiled to Rangoon, where he died in 1862.

Q.No 39 HistoryEnglish

A
1707
✓ Correct
B
1717
C
1747
D
1757

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Mughal emperor Aurangzeb died in 1707 (at Ahmednagar), an event that is often taken to mark the beginning of the decline of the Mughal Empire.

Q.No 38 HistoryEnglish

A
1767-69
✓ Correct
B
1780-84
C
1790-92
D
1799

Solution

Correct answer: A. The First Anglo-Mysore War was fought in 1767-1769 between the British East India Company and Haidar Ali of Mysore, ending with the Treaty of Madras.

Q.No 39 HistoryEnglish
Who among the following was never the Nawab of Bengal?
A
Alivardi Khan
B
Saadat Ali Khan
✓ Correct
C
Mir Qasim
D
Sirajuddaulah

Solution

Correct answer: B. Saadat Ali Khan was the Nawab of Awadh (Oudh), not of Bengal, so he was never the Nawab of Bengal.

Q.No 40 HistoryEnglish
Henry Lawrence was the Chief Commissioner of which state during the Revolt of 1857?
A
Awadh
✓ Correct
B
Odisha
C
Maharashtra
D
Punjab

Solution

Correct answer: A. Sir Henry Lawrence was the Chief Commissioner of Awadh (Oudh) during the Revolt of 1857 and died defending the Residency at Lucknow.

B is incorrect: Odisha was not the region of Henry Lawrence's command.

C is incorrect: Maharashtra was not under his charge during the revolt.

D is incorrect: Punjab was administered by his brother John Lawrence; Henry was posted to Awadh.

💡 The Siege of the Lucknow Residency was one of the most famous episodes of the 1857 Revolt.
