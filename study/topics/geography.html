असम
B
ओड़िशा
✓ Correct
C
त्रिपुरा
D
पश्चिम बंगाल

Solution

Correct answer: B. Odisha does not share a border with Bangladesh. Bangladesh borders the Indian states of West Bengal, Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura and Mizoram.

Q.No 26 GeographyHindi
मालवा पठार मुख्यतः: किस राज्य में स्थित है?
A
मध्य प्रदेश
✓ Correct
B
महाराष्ट्र
C
राजस्थान
D
गुजरात

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Malwa Plateau lies mainly in Madhya Pradesh (extending into parts of Rajasthan and Gujarat) and is a volcanic, black-soil plateau in west-central India.

B is incorrect: Maharashtra is associated with the Deccan Plateau, not the Malwa Plateau.

C is incorrect: Only the north-western fringe of Malwa touches Rajasthan; the plateau is principally in MP.

D is incorrect: Gujarat lies to the west and holds only a small edge of the region.

💡 The Malwa Plateau is drained by rivers like the Chambal and Betwa and is known for its fertile black soil.
Q.No 27 GeographyHindi⚠ low OCR confidence
भाबर क्षेत्र भारत की किस भौतिक विशेषता से जुड़ा हुआ है?
A
हिमालय की तलहटी
✓ Correct
B
प्रायद्वीपीय पठार
C
सिंधु-गंगा का मैदान
D
रेगिस्तानी क्षेत्र

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Bhabar is a narrow belt of porous, pebbly soil at the foothills of the Himalayas, where rivers descending from the mountains deposit coarse sediments and streams often disappear underground.

B is incorrect: The Peninsular Plateau is an ancient landmass of the south, unrelated to the Bhabar.

C is incorrect: The Indo-Gangetic plain lies south of the Bhabar; the Terai (not Bhabar) adjoins it.

D is incorrect: The Bhabar is a humid foothill zone, not a desert region.

💡 Just south of the Bhabar lies the marshy Terai belt, where the underground streams re-emerge.

A
हल्दिया
B
कोयली
C
बरौनी
D
डिगबोई
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. India's first oil refinery was established at Digboi in Assam (commissioned in 1901), making it one of the oldest operating refineries in the world.

B is incorrect: Koyali (Gujarat) refinery came up in the 1960s.

Q.No 25 GeographyHindi
सतपुड़ा पर्वतमाला भारत के किस भाग में स्थित है?
A
उत्तरी भारत
B
दक्षिणी भारत
C
मध्य भारत
✓ Correct
D
पूर्वी भारत

Solution

Correct answer: C. The Satpura Range runs east-west through central India (Madhya Pradesh and adjoining areas), lying between the Narmada and Tapti rivers.

A is incorrect: Northern India is dominated by the Himalayas and the Gangetic plain, not the Satpura.

B is incorrect: Southern India has the Deccan Plateau and the Western/Eastern Ghats.

D is incorrect: Eastern India has the Chotanagpur Plateau and eastern hills, not the Satpura.

💡 The Satpura Range separates the Narmada valley to its north from the Tapti valley to its south.
Q.No 26 GeographyHindi
गंगा-ब्रह्मपुत्र डेल्टा मुख्यतः: किस राज्य में स्थित है?
A
पश्चिम बंगाल
✓ Correct
B
उत्तर प्रदेश
C
बिहार
D
असाम

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Ganga-Brahmaputra delta lies mainly in West Bengal (and Bangladesh); within India it is located in West Bengal and forms the Sundarbans.

B is incorrect: Uttar Pradesh lies in the upper Ganga plain, not the delta.

C is incorrect: Bihar is in the middle Ganga plain, upstream of the delta.

D is incorrect: Assam lies along the Brahmaputra valley, not the combined delta.

💡 The Ganga-Brahmaputra delta is the world's largest delta and hosts the Sundarbans mangrove forest.
Q.No 36 GeographyHindi
भारत में खारे पानी की सबसे बड़ी झील कौन-सी है?
A
वेम्बनाड झील
B
पुलिकट झील
C
चिल्‍का झील
✓ Correct
D
सांभर झील

Solution

Correct answer: C. Chilika Lake in Odisha is India's largest brackish (saltwater) lake and the largest coastal lagoon in the country.

A is incorrect: Vembanad Lake (Kerala) is the longest lake in India but Chilika is the largest brackish-water lake.

B is incorrect: Pulicat Lake is the second-largest brackish lagoon, smaller than Chilika.

D is incorrect: Sambhar Lake (Rajasthan) is the largest inland salt (saline) lake, but Chilika is the largest brackish coastal lake.

💡 Chilika is a Ramsar wetland famous for migratory birds and the Irrawaddy dolphin.
Q.No 26 GeographyEnglish
The Sunderbans Delta is formed by the confluence of which rivers?
A
Ganga and Yamuna
B
Ganga and Brahmaputra
✓ Correct
C
Godavari and Krishna
D
Mahanadi and Godavari

Solution

Correct answer: B. The Sundarbans delta is formed by the confluence of the Ganga, Brahmaputra and Meghana rivers; among the options, the Ganga and Brahmaputra are the principal rivers.

A is incorrect: The Yamuna is a tributary of the Ganga and does not directly form the Sundarbans delta.

C is incorrect: The Godavari and Krishna form deltas on the eastern (Andhra) coast.

D is incorrect: The Mahanadi and Godavari form separate deltas, not the Sundarbans.

💡 The Sundarbans is the world's largest mangrove forest and home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.
Q.No 27 GeographyEnglish
The Himalayas are wider in which sector of India?
A
Western Himalayas
✓ Correct
B
Central Himalayas
C
Eastern Himalayas
D
Shiwalik Himalayas

Solution

Correct answer: A. The Himalayas are widest in the Western (Kashmir) sector and progressively narrow towards the east; the western Himalayas span the greatest north-south width.

B is incorrect: The Central Himalayas (Nepal region) are narrower than the western sector.

C is incorrect: The Eastern Himalayas are the narrowest but rise very steeply.

D is incorrect: The Shiwaliks are only the outermost, lowest range, not a 'wider' sector of the whole system.

💡 The Himalayas are divided into the Western, Central and Eastern sectors, and the highest peak, Mount Everest, lies in the Central (Nepal) Himalayas.
