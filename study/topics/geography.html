Q.No 27 GeographyEnglish
Which of the following plate boundaries formed the Himalayas?
A
Divergent boundary
B
Transform boundary
C
Hotspot boundary
D
Convergent boundary
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. The Himalayas were formed at a convergent plate boundary, where the Indian Plate collided with and pushed under the Eurasian Plate.

A is incorrect: At a divergent boundary plates move apart, forming rifts and mid-ocean ridges.

B is incorrect: At a transform boundary plates slide past each other (e.g. the San Andreas Fault).

C is incorrect: A hotspot creates volcanic chains (like Hawaii), not fold mountains like the Himalayas.

💡 The Indian Plate is still pushing north, so the Himalayas continue to rise by a few millimetres each year.
