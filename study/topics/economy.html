Q.No 27 EconomyEnglish
The Ease of Doing Business (EODB) indicator “Enforcing Contracts” measures:
A
implementation of labor regulations
B
Ease of paying and managing taxes
C
Efficiency of the judicial system in resolving commercial disputes
✓ Correct
D
Effectiveness of law enforcement agencies

Solution

Correct answer: C. In the World Bank's Ease of Doing Business framework, the 'Enforcing Contracts' indicator measures the time, cost and procedural quality of the judicial system in resolving a standardised commercial dispute.

A is incorrect: Labour-regulation implementation is captured by separate labour-market indicators, not Enforcing Contracts.

B is incorrect: Ease of paying and managing taxes is measured by the 'Paying Taxes' indicator.

D is incorrect: Effectiveness of law-enforcement (police) agencies is a governance/security measure, not part of this EODB indicator.

💡 The World Bank discontinued the Doing Business report in 2021 and is replacing it with the Business Ready (B-READY) project.
Q.No 31 EconomyEnglish
What was the main drawback of small-scale industry reservation policies?
A
They encouraged monopoly power
B
They eliminated export opportunities
C
They excluded rural entrepreneurs
D
They restricted economies of scale and technological upgrading
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. Reserving products exclusively for small-scale industries kept firms artificially small, which restricted economies of scale and discouraged investment in modern technology, hurting productivity and competitiveness.

A is incorrect: The policy was meant to prevent monopoly by protecting small firms, not to encourage monopoly power.

B is incorrect: It did not specifically eliminate export opportunities; the core problem was scale and technology.

C is incorrect: Reservation did not exclude rural entrepreneurs; in fact small units were widely spread.

💡 India progressively 'de-reserved' SSI products after 1997, and the reservation list was abolished entirely by 2015.
Q.No 32 EconomyEnglish
Which was the model for the First Five-Year Plan (1951-1956)?
A
Solow model
B
Harrod-Domar model
✓ Correct
C
Keynesian model
D
Mahalanobis model

Solution

Correct answer: B. India's First Five-Year Plan (1951-56) was based on the Harrod-Domar model, which links growth to the rate of saving and investment and the capital-output ratio.

A is incorrect: The Solow growth model came later and was not the basis of the First Plan.

C is incorrect: The Keynesian model focuses on demand management, not the planning framework used here.

D is incorrect: The Mahalanobis model was the basis of the Second Five-Year Plan (1956-61), not the First.

💡 The First Plan prioritised agriculture and irrigation following the disruptions of Partition.

Solution

Q.No 35 EconomyHindi
जब नाममात्र सकल घरेलू उत्पाद (८07?) वास्तविक जीडीपी (८0?) की तुलना में तेज़ी से बढ़ता है, तो यह __ दर्शाता है
A
शून्य वृद्धि
B
गिरती मुद्रास्फीति
C
उच्च मुद्रास्फीति
✓ Correct
D
अपस्फीति

Solution

Correct answer: C. If nominal GDP rises faster than real GDP, the gap is due to rising prices - this indicates high inflation, because nominal GDP is measured at current prices while real GDP is adjusted for price changes.

B is incorrect: Falling inflation would narrow, not widen, the gap between nominal and real GDP.

D is incorrect: Deflation means falling prices, which would make nominal GDP grow slower than real GDP.

💡 The ratio of nominal to real GDP is the GDP deflator, a broad measure of economy-wide inflation.

Q.No 36 EconomyEnglish
Which index measures wholesale prices?
A
Gross Domestic Product Deflator
B
Consumer Price Index
C
Index of Industrial Production
D
Wholesale Price Index
✓ Correct

Solution

Correct answer: D. The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) measures the average change in prices of goods at the wholesale level (bulk transactions between businesses).

A is incorrect: The GDP deflator measures economy-wide price change, not specifically wholesale prices.

Q.No 37 EconomyEnglish
Which structural constraint continues to be the most critical factor limiting agricultural productivity in India?
A
inadequacy of fertile soils across major regions
B
High dependence on monsoonal variability
✓ Correct
C
Chronic shortage of agricultural labor
D
Weakness of domestic market linkages

Solution

Correct answer: B. High dependence on the monsoon (rainfall variability) is the most critical structural constraint on Indian agricultural productivity, since a large share of farmland is rain-fed and vulnerable to erratic rains.

A is incorrect: India has substantial fertile soil; inadequacy of fertile soil is not the principal constraint.

C is incorrect: India generally has surplus agricultural labour, not a chronic shortage.

D is incorrect: Weak market linkages are a problem but not the most critical productivity constraint.

💡 About half of India's net sown area is still rain-fed, which is why irrigation expansion is a policy priority.
