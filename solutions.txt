------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Which body recommends the distribution of taxes between Center and States?
   A) Planning Commission
   B) Finance Commission  <-- CORRECT
   C) Parliament
   D) NITI Aayog
Answer: B
Why correct: The Finance Commission, a constitutional body under Article 280, is constituted every five years by the President to recommend how the net proceeds of taxes should be divided (vertical devolution) between the Union and the States, and how funds should be distributed among the States (horizontal devolution).
Why A is wrong: The Planning Commission (1950-2014) prepared Five-Year Plans and allocated plan assistance, but it had no role in dividing tax revenue and is now defunct.
Why C is wrong: Parliament enacts tax laws and passes the Budget, but it does not recommend the formula for sharing tax proceeds with the States.
Why D is wrong: NITI Aayog is only an advisory think-tank that replaced the Planning Commission; it has no constitutional power over tax devolution.
Note: The 15th Finance Commission (chaired by N.K. Singh) recommended a 41% share of the divisible pool for the States.

Q: When is acceleration considered positive?
   A) When it is opposite to velocity
   B) When velocity is decreasing
   C) When it is in the direction of velocity  <-- CORRECT
   D) When the object is at rest
Answer: C
Why correct: Acceleration is taken as positive when it acts in the same direction as the velocity, because the body then speeds up in its direction of motion.
Why A is wrong: When acceleration is opposite to velocity it is negative acceleration (retardation/deceleration).
Why B is wrong: Decreasing velocity means the acceleration opposes motion, i.e. it is negative.
Why D is wrong: An object at rest with no change in velocity has zero acceleration.
Note: Acceleration is a vector quantity, so its sign depends on the chosen positive direction relative to the velocity.

   A) 15 days
   B) 58 days  <-- CORRECT

Why C is wrong: 100 days corresponds to roughly 17 km/s.
Why D is wrong: 365 days (one year) corresponds to a speed of under 5 km/s.
Note: 1 AU is the average Earth-Sun distance; light covers it in about 8 minutes 20 seconds.

Q: Archimedes’ principle states that a body immersed in a fluid experiences an upward force equal to
   A) Its own weight
   B) The volume of the body
   C) The weight of the fluid displaced  <-- CORRECT
   D) The density of the fluid
Answer: C
Why correct: Archimedes' principle states that a body immersed (fully or partly) in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces.
Why A is wrong: The buoyant force equals the displaced fluid's weight, not the body's own weight (these are equal only when the body floats).
Why B is wrong: The volume of the body is a quantity, not a force.
Why D is wrong: Density of the fluid alone is not a force; the upthrust depends on weight of fluid displaced.
Note: This principle explains why ships float and why objects feel lighter in water.

Q: if an astronaut’s mass is 70 kg, what will be their weight on the Moon (1/6th of Earth's gravity)?
   A) 700 N
   B) 117 N  <-- CORRECT
   C) 420 N
   D) 686 N
Answer: B
Why correct: Weight on the Moon = mass x lunar gravity = 70 kg x (9.8/6) m/s^2 ≈ 70 x 1.63 ≈ 114-117 N.
Why A is wrong: 700 N (using g = 10) would be the approximate weight on Earth, not the Moon.
Why C is wrong: 420 N would imply gravity far higher than one-sixth of Earth's.
Why D is wrong: 686 N (70 x 9.8) is the weight on Earth, not on the Moon.
Note: Mass stays constant everywhere; only weight (= mass x local gravity) changes between the Earth and the Moon.
